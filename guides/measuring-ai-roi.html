Measuring AI ROI
The framework

Three places AI returns money

Every automation pays back in one of three ways. Add them up and you have a clear, honest ROI you can defend to anyone.

1

Time saved

Hours your team no longer spends on manual work. Multiply hours saved per month by their hourly cost.

2

Revenue gained

Deals you win that you used to lose, faster replies, no dropped leads, more follow-up. Count the extra closed revenue.

3

Cost avoided

Hires you didn't need to make, errors you didn't have to fix, tools you consolidated. Real money that stays in the business.

Track the inputs, not just the outcome

Pick 2–3 numbers per automation (e.g. response time, leads contacted, hours logged) and measure them before and after. The before/after gap is your proof.

Measuring AI ROI
The math

Put a number on it

ROI = (Time saved + Revenue gained + Cost avoided − Cost of the system) ÷ Cost of the system
Measured monthly, then annualised.

Worked example. Say an automation saves 25 hours a month at an effective cost of $20/hour ($500), recovers 2 deals worth $1,500, and avoids one part-time hire's overhead ($800). That's $2,800 of monthly value. If the system cost $2,000 to build and $150/month to run, it pays for itself inside the first month, then returns roughly 18× its monthly running cost every month after.

Be conservative

Use cautious numbers. An automation that clearly wins on careful estimates is far more convincing, and far more likely to keep delivering, than one that only works on optimistic ones.
